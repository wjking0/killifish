    Porridge Cultured Vinegar Eels

    I have to thank Alberto Reis for this technique

    There are 2 great and related reasons to use vinegar eels over say microworms.

    1. They swim at all levels in the water column. This means that killifish fry, which aren’t natural bottom dwellers, can happily munch on a passing worm without digging in the dirt.
    2. Vinegar eels are happy in liquid and survive for a long-time swimming around in a sinusoidal manner. I suspect many of the Microworms we feed end up in the stomachs of our snails rather than our fry. I guess it’s also possible Microworms are less well adapted to survive in a liquid than vinegar eels are.

    Method

    1. boil the kettle.
    2. add about 25 mls of vinegar into a measuring jug.
    3. Add an equal volume of the boiled water to this.
    4. microwave to heat it all back up again.
    5. Pour this over some porridge oats in a margarine tub until the oats are reasonably damp.
    6. Allow to cool.
    7. Add your vinegar eel starter
